The Danish Diabetes Association

Visit the finished product

The task

The Danish diabetes association is one of many organisations that aims to aid and easy the lives of diabetics and their families. One of their ideas was to create a web app that would have their shopping guide digitalised so people wouldn’t have to carry a physical copy on themselves everywhere they go.

The beginning

The first thing we did as a team was to establish what needed to be done in order to create a web app that the people will love because of its usefulness, looks and usability. To accomplish all these things, we needed to understand the users (diabetics, their friends and families) – what they needed in addition to the guide to make their everyday lives easier.

User Research

The first step we took was to asses the association’s values and the user’s needs and according to them to create a well-planned interview questions so we can get a better understanding of the problem and its solution. In addition to what we already knew we had to do(digitalize the guide) we found that one of the most common problems that diabetics faced in their everyday lives were diversity in their diet and keeping track of their carbohydrates, sugars and glucose levels for the day.

Information architecture

Later the gathered data was turned into a requirement specification which was going to guide us through out the development of the project. It also helped us to establish the Information architecture (IA) of the web-app. In the end we ended up with 5 main pages with their relevant information(Home – contains the two digitalized versions of the shopping guide with the purpose of easy access and instant understanding of the purpose of the web-app, Recipes – the place where people can bring changes into their meals and save their favorite ones, Diary – where people could log their day, meals and mood, Shopping list – where they could always keep tabs on their ingredients either for their favorite recipes or the meal they are planning to cook and Profile – the place where all favorite recipes and diary logs could be reviewed.)

Design

When the IA was finished, we moved onto the design. The trust and feeling between the user and the official site should be kept at all cost and that’s why we stuck to the association’s color scheme. That way, we made the transition between the two sites seemingless and effortless. Through out the design we stuck with famous design patterns and icons so when in use, the usability and experience will be optimal. They will be familiar with the layout and interactions from other sites and apps so no tutorials will be needed. Further more, to achieve the app feeling in the browser we performed some benchmarking against one of the most famous platforms like Facebook, Instagram, etc. from which we concluded that the feeling will be achieved through placing the main navigation at the bottom with its relevant icons and just in case – labels.

Implementation and coding

And finally – the code! Since in apps you don’t get redirected to other places, we knew that we should replicate that as well. So, we ended up with basically a one-pager which only by the press of a menu button to be manipulated with JavaScript and CSS to achieve the feeling of an app but in the browser. One drawback was the ‘back’ function of the browser which returned you to the last page according to your browsing history. To fix that, we implements tabs (design pattern;) on the home page for an easy switch between the two versions of the shopping guide and also the well-know ‘back-arrow’ in the places where the user would go in to explore the web-app and then need to go back where he came from.
