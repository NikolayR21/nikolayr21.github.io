Ungdomsringen Musikfestival Website

Visit the finished product

The client

Ungdomsringen is an organization which purpose is to help young people establish significant relationships, become part of communities and most importantly – bring wholeness in the lives of children and young people. They achieve this through their support of youth clubs and schools in Denmark and also through organizing events for the youth to participate in. Ungdomsringens musikfestival is one of them and is one the biggest festivals in the Nordic region.

The task

As every big event, the music festival has its content spread across multiple platforms. That is a problem for them and their attendants, both participant and audiences, because it causes a mix of information and a feeling of uncertainty in the attendants. They gave us the task to create a durable website, which they could update themselves and also through which they could express their values.

User research & understanding

First, to generate a design and content that would be appealing to the users, we needed to get in their heads – what’s the most important thing that they look for when they want to attend a festival, how do they expect to find it, etc. In the end we concluded that the most important things for them were the schedule and the artists, the rest barely mattered. But since we had requirements from the client as well, we came with a compromise between the two.

Requirement Specification

To fully satisfy both sides and stick to that goal, we created a requirement specification that contained all the things we needed to do in order to achieve this – a fully-responsive durable website, that would allow the client to update the content easily by themselves and also a design and information architecture that would easily express their values and show the user where they stand.

Information architecture & more

When moving onto the information architecture we began by performing competitive benchmarks against similar and popular festivals. From that we concluded that they all have a Home page where briefly present themselves and mention the time and place of the events, Line up page with the dates and hours of the performances, a page about all the artists where they could find out a little more about who the artist is and a generic information page that includes information about accommodation, security, food and etc. If people are familiar already with this type of information architecture and its proven to work, we decided to do the same.

The design

They didn’t have much of an established color scheme so the design was purely based on our research and on what people most often associate festivals with – vibrant colors, movement, dynamics. That being said, our design consisted of vibrant neon colored lines with animations, pictures and videos showing people having a good time and dynamic interactions with some elements. The content had to be short and clear because not many people paid much attention to it except for the schedule.

The code

We worked with WordPress’ API to fuel the websites content. We set up categories and tags which the client would use when adding new content or updating it, consisting of custom field groups with relevant Titles and fields. Later we would use AJAX to take that content and turn it into a readable by JavaScript format(JSON) that we can implement into the document when the page is being loaded. That way the site’s content and possibly looks could be edited by the client without the need of a developer.
